An open Bible, anchored to Bitcoin.

OpenNeo is a peer-to-peer Scripture protocol — Neo Ontology Scripture (NeoOS), open source. Every verse is content-addressed, every correction is anchored to Bitcoin, and translators are paid in Lightning, with no gatekeeper over the text.

Tools for every reader

Whether you want to read Scripture, correct it, or embed it anywhere, there is an OpenNeo tool for you — all open, all verifiable.

OpenNeo Reader
Read NeoOS — the 87-book canon, offline in your browser, with change history and Bitcoin anchors.
Translator Portal
Propose, review, merge + Bitcoin anchor — the full correction loop. Council governance gates every merge.
Cite SDK
Open & verifiable — embed any verse on any site; each citation is a free, signed use-proof.

OpenNeo is an open, peer-to-peer system for Scripture. It lays the foundation for a Bible no platform can alter and no gatekeeper can own.

How it fits together

Four interlocking layers

A specialization of Nostr for Scripture, with Bitcoin for permanence and Lightning for payments.

NeoOS

Neo Ontology Scripture — an open source, accuracy-first English corpus (85 of 87 books today), Hebrew names, every verse content-addressed with BLAKE3.

Relay Protocol

Content-addressed events on Nostr relays. Anyone runs a relay; clients pull from many, push to many. Council governance keeps merges Sybil-resistant.

Bitcoin Translation Protocol

Immutable PR history anchored to Bitcoin via OpenTimestamps. Translators paid in Lightning on merge, with signed receipts and peer review.

OpenNeo Reader

PWA + CLI: read, see change history and Bitcoin anchors, propose and vote on revisions.
